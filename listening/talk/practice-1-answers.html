Talk 1 — Honeybee Communication (Life Science)

話者：教授 ｜ Standard

スクリプトを見る（シグナルワードをハイライト付き）
Today, I want to talk about one of the most fascinating forms of animal communication — the waggle dance of honeybees. When a forager bee discovers a good source of nectar or pollen, it returns to the hive and performs a special dance to tell other bees exactly where to find it. The bee moves in a figure-eight pattern while waggling its body. The direction of the waggle run indicates the angle of the food source relative to the sun. If the bee waggles straight up on the honeycomb, it means the food is in the direction of the sun. The duration of the waggle is equally important — the longer the bee dances, the farther away the food source is. This remarkable behavior was first decoded by the Austrian scientist Karl von Frisch, who spent decades studying bee communication. His groundbreaking research earned him the Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine in 1973. What makes this especially interesting is that it shows us that even insects with tiny brains can communicate precise information in ways that scientists once thought were far too complex for them.
1
What is the talk mainly about?
Main Idea
(A) The life cycle of honeybees.
(B) The history of beekeeping.
(C) How honeybees communicate the location of food sources.
(D) How bees produce honey.
解説： 冒頭で「I want to talk about ... the waggle dance of honeybees」と主題を明示。全体を通してミツバチのコミュニケーション方法に焦点を当てている。(A)ライフサイクル、(B)養蜂の歴史、(D)ハチミツの生産は言及なし。
🔑 Main Idea問題：冒頭の「I want to talk about...」がそのまま答え。★シグナルワード直後に注目。
2
Why does the speaker mention Karl von Frisch?
Purpose
(A) To explain why bees are endangered.
(B) To compare different types of animal communication.
(C) To introduce a new theory about insect behavior.
(D) To credit the scientist who discovered the waggle dance.
解説： Karl von Frischはワグルダンスを「decoded（解読）」した科学者として紹介されている。彼の業績を認め、ノーベル賞受賞という文脈で言及。(A)絶滅危機、(B)比較、(C)新理論は言及なし。
🔑 Purpose問題のコツ：「Why does the speaker mention X?」→ Xの前後の文脈を見る。ここでは研究の功績を紹介する目的。
3
According to the speaker, what does the duration of the waggle dance indicate?
Detail
(A) The distance to the food source.
(B) The number of bees needed to collect the food.
(C) The quality of the food source.
(D) The type of flower producing the nectar.
解説： 講師は「the longer the bee dances, the farther away the food source is」と明確に述べている。ダンスの持続時間＝食料源までの距離。(B)(C)(D)は言及なし。
🔑 Detail問題：キーワード「duration」「longer」に注目。本文中の対応箇所を正確に聞き取る。
4
What can be inferred from the talk?
Inference
(A) Honeybees can only communicate through dancing.
(B) Bee communication is more sophisticated than previously thought.
(C) All insects use the waggle dance.
(D) Karl von Frisch trained bees to perform the dance.
解説： 講師は最後に「even insects with tiny brains can communicate precise information in ways that scientists once thought were far too complex for them」と述べている。ミツバチのコミュニケーションが以前考えられていたよりも高度であることが示唆されている。(A)「only」は極端、(C)全昆虫とは述べていない、(D)解読であり教えたのではない。
🔑 Inference問題：直接述べていないが論理的に導ける結論を選ぶ。最後の文に注目。

Talk 2 — The Greenhouse Effect (Physical Science)

話者：教授 ｜ Harder

スクリプトを見る（シグナルワードをハイライト付き）
Let's discuss a topic that's critically important for understanding our planet's climate — the greenhouse effect. Now, first of all, the greenhouse effect is not inherently a bad thing. In fact, without it, the Earth's average surface temperature would be about minus 18 degrees Celsius, far too cold to support life as we know it.

Here's how it works: solar radiation from the sun passes through the atmosphere and warms the Earth's surface. The Earth then radiates heat back toward space. However, certain gases in the atmosphere — called greenhouse gases — absorb some of this outgoing heat and re-emit it in all directions, including back toward the surface. This is the natural greenhouse effect, and the main greenhouse gases involved are carbon dioxide, methane, and water vapor.

Now, here's where the problem arises. Human activities have been dramatically increasing the concentration of these greenhouse gases, particularly carbon dioxide and methane. When we burn fossil fuels like coal, oil, and natural gas for energy, we release enormous amounts of CO2 into the atmosphere. Deforestation makes the problem worse because trees naturally absorb CO2 during photosynthesis. When we cut down forests, not only do we lose that absorption capacity, but the carbon stored in the trees is released back into the atmosphere.

This enhanced greenhouse effect is what scientists refer to when they talk about global warming and climate change. The result is a gradual increase in global temperatures, which leads to melting ice caps, rising sea levels, and more extreme weather events.
5
What is the talk mainly about?
Main Idea
(A) The greenhouse effect and its impact on climate.
(B) The history of fossil fuel usage.
(C) Methods of reducing carbon emissions.
(D) The formation of the Earth's atmosphere.
解説： 講義全体が温室効果の仕組み（自然な温室効果と人為的な強化温室効果）とその気候への影響を説明している。(B)化石燃料の歴史は部分的、(C)排出削減の方法は未議論、(D)大気の形成は言及なし。
🔑 Main Idea問題：冒頭の「Let's discuss...」がそのまま答え。
6
According to the speaker, what is the natural greenhouse effect?
Detail
(A) A process that makes the Earth too hot for life.
(B) A recent phenomenon caused by human activity.
(C) A process that keeps the Earth warm enough to support life.
(D) A process that only affects the polar regions.
解説： 講師は「without it, the Earth's average surface temperature would be about minus 18 degrees Celsius, far too cold to support life」と述べている。自然な温室効果は生命維持に必要な温暖さを保つプロセス。(A)暑くしすぎるは逆、(B)最近の現象ではない、(D)極地だけではない。
🔑 定義問題：「the greenhouse effect is not inherently a bad thing」の直後に理由が続く。
7
Why does the speaker mention deforestation?
Purpose
(A) To describe how new forests are being planted.
(B) To explain a human activity that increases CO2 levels.
(C) To compare forests in different regions.
(D) To discuss the lumber industry's economic impact.
解説： 講師は森林伐採について「trees naturally absorb CO2」「when we cut down forests...the carbon stored in the trees is released back into the atmosphere」と説明。CO2濃度を上昇させる人間活動の具体例として言及。(A)(C)(D)は言及なし。
🔑 Purpose問題：「Deforestation makes the problem worse because...」と因果関係で接続されている。
8
Which of the following is NOT mentioned as a greenhouse gas?
NOT/EXCEPT
(A) Carbon dioxide.
(B) Methane.
(C) Water vapor.
(D) Oxygen.
解説： 講師が挙げた温室効果ガスは carbon dioxide、methane、water vapor の3つ。Oxygen（酸素）は温室効果ガスとして言及されていない。NOT問題は消去法で解く。
🔑 NOT/EXCEPT問題の鉄則：消去法で解く。本文に言及のある3つを消し、残った1つが正解。
