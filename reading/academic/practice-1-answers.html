Academic Reading Strategy Tips
1 Correct answers are always paraphrases
The correct answer restates the passage in different words. Be suspicious of options that copy exact words from the passage — they are often traps.
Paraphrase examples from this practice:
Passage: "track goods, debts, and transactions" → Answer: "keep track of goods and commercial exchanges"
Passage: "play a crucial role in regulating host populations" → Answer: "help maintain balance by controlling the size of host populations"
2 Understand passage structure
First sentence → Topic / Main claim
Middle → Details, examples, data
Last sentence → Conclusion / Summary

Main Idea questions can often be solved by reading just the first and last sentences.
Q1: Passage 1 opens with "The invention of writing is widely regarded as one of the most significant achievements" and ends with "they all followed a comparable path: from pictures, to symbols for words, to symbols for sounds." Together these give the main idea: how writing systems originated and evolved = A.
3 Inference must come from the text only
Do not mix in your own knowledge or opinions. Only information stated in the passage counts as evidence.
Most common mistake:
✓ Passage says "parasites regulate host populations" → "they help maintain balance" (valid inference from the text)
✗ Passage says "parasites regulate host populations" → "they are beneficial to the hosts" (unsupported — the passage says they cause damage)
4 Main Idea: first + last sentences are key
For Main Idea questions, do not get distracted by specific details in the middle paragraphs. The correct answer covers the entire passage, not just one section.
Trap pattern: An option that accurately describes one paragraph but ignores the rest of the passage. This is a detail, not the main idea.
The Development of Writing Systems History
Question 1 — Main Idea
What is the passage mainly about?
Correct: A Your answer: —
A How early writing systems originated and evolved across different civilizations ✓
B The reasons why cuneiform was replaced by alphabetic writing
C A comparison of the literary traditions of Mesopotamia, Egypt, and China
D The role of agriculture in the decline of ancient civilizations
Structure approach: Paragraph 1 introduces cuneiform as the earliest writing system and its practical origins. Paragraph 2 describes how it evolved from pictographs to sound-based symbols. Paragraph 3 shows similar development in Egypt and China. The thread connecting all paragraphs is how writing systems originated and evolved = A.

B is wrong because the passage never discusses cuneiform being replaced. C mentions "literary traditions," but the passage focuses on the development of writing, not literature. D mentions agriculture only as context for why record-keeping was needed; it is not the main topic.
Paraphrase: Passage says "emerged," "evolved," "arose in different cultures" → A says "originated and evolved across different civilizations"
Question 2 — Vocabulary in Context
The word "abstract" in paragraph 2 is closest in meaning to
Correct: B Your answer: —
A colorful
B simplified and non-representational ✓
C difficult to produce
D permanently recorded
Context check: The passage says pictographs "became increasingly abstract" and the next sentence explains that writers switched to "wedge-shaped marks" that were "faster to produce than detailed drawings." This means the symbols moved away from being realistic pictures and became simplified, non-representational shapes = B.

A (colorful) has no support in context. C is the opposite — the passage says they were faster to produce. D (permanently recorded) describes the clay tablets, not the nature of the symbols.
Question 3 — Factual Detail
According to the passage, why was cuneiform originally developed?
Correct: D Your answer: —
A To write down religious prayers and rituals
B To communicate with neighboring civilizations
C To record the history of ancient kings
D To keep track of goods and commercial exchanges ✓
Explanation: Paragraph 1 explicitly states cuneiform "was initially developed not for literary or religious purposes but for a far more practical reason: record-keeping" and that "people needed a reliable method to track goods, debts, and transactions" = D.

A is directly contradicted — the passage says it was not for religious purposes. B and C are not mentioned as original purposes.
Paraphrase: Passage: "track goods, debts, and transactions" → D: "keep track of goods and commercial exchanges" — "transactions" is paraphrased as "commercial exchanges"
Symbiotic Relationships in Marine Ecosystems Life Science
Question 4 — Rhetorical Purpose
Why does the author mention clownfish and sea anemones in paragraph 2?
Correct: C Your answer: —
A To explain why anemones are dangerous to most marine animals
B To argue that clownfish are the most important species in coral reefs
C To provide an example of a mutually beneficial relationship ✓
D To compare mutualism with parasitism
Explanation: Paragraph 2 opens with "Mutualism is a relationship in which both species benefit" and then introduces the clownfish-anemone example with "One of the most well-known examples." The example serves to illustrate mutualism = C.

A focuses on only one detail (stinging tentacles) and misses the purpose. B uses extreme language ("most important") with no support. D is wrong because parasitism is discussed in a separate paragraph; this paragraph is solely about mutualism.
Question 5 — Vocabulary in Context
The word "thrive" in paragraph 2 is closest in meaning to
Correct: B Your answer: —
A survive with difficulty
B grow and develop successfully ✓
C compete aggressively
D move freely
Context check: The sentence reads "Neither organism would thrive as well without the other," describing how both benefit from the relationship. "Thrive" means to do well, to grow and develop successfully = B. Substituting: "Neither organism would grow and develop successfully as well without the other" — this reads naturally.

A is the opposite — "survive with difficulty" implies struggle, not success. C (compete) and D (move freely) do not fit the context of mutual benefit.
Question 6 — Inference
Based on the passage, what can be inferred about the role of parasites in marine ecosystems?
Correct: D Your answer: —
A They are always harmful and serve no ecological purpose.
B They are becoming less common due to rising ocean temperatures.
C They primarily affect plants rather than animals in the ocean.
D They help maintain balance by controlling the size of host populations. ✓
Text-based inference: The final paragraph states that "parasites play a crucial role in regulating host populations and preventing any single species from becoming overly dominant." From this, we can infer that parasites help maintain ecosystem balance by controlling population sizes = D.

A directly contradicts the passage — ecologists argue parasites DO play "a crucial role." The word "always" is also an extreme qualifier that should raise suspicion. B introduces information (ocean temperatures) not discussed anywhere in the passage. C is unsupported — the passage only mentions fish as hosts.
Paraphrase: Passage: "play a crucial role in regulating host populations" → D: "help maintain balance by controlling the size of host populations" — "regulating" is paraphrased as "controlling the size of"
Key Takeaways
1. Correct answers are always paraphrases of the passage. Options using the same words as the passage are often traps.
2. For inference questions, only the passage counts as evidence. Do not mix in your own knowledge or opinions.
3. Main Idea = first sentence + last sentence. Do not get pulled into middle-paragraph details.
4. Vocabulary in Context: substitute and check. Replace the word with each option and see which one reads most naturally.
