Read in Daily Life Strategy Tips
1 Identify the purpose from opening keywords
The first 1-2 sentences reveal the purpose of the text. Learn the patterns:

Notification: We are pleased to inform... / We would like to inform you...
Request: Could you... / I would appreciate if...
Confirmation: I'm writing to confirm...
Apology: I apologize for... / Unfortunately...
Q1: The email opens with "Your pre-ordered textbooks are now available for pickup" and then describes pickup rules and exchange policies. This is an informational notification = B.
2 NOT/EXCEPT: Use elimination
Steps:
1. Circle NOT/EXCEPT in the question
2. Check each option against the passage one by one
3. Cross out the 3 options that ARE mentioned in the passage. The remaining one is the answer.
Common traps:
- A word from the passage appears in the option, but the meaning is different
- Partially correct but inaccurate as a whole
- States the opposite of what the passage says
3 Inference questions: stick to the text
For inference questions, the answer must be logically supported by information in the passage. Do not mix in your own knowledge or assumptions.
Q4: The passage says the laundry room "will remain open" but the south entrance "will be restricted" and residents should "use the north entrance instead." This directly supports B.
Passage 1 Email
Question 1 — Main Purpose
What is the main purpose of this email?
Correct: B Your answer: —
A To advertise new textbooks available at the bookstore
B To inform students about textbook pickup procedures and policies ✓
C To remind students to pay their tuition fees before the semester starts
D To explain how to order textbooks online for the first time
Explanation: The email body tells students their pre-orders are ready, explains what to bring for pickup, provides the pickup dates, and describes the exchange/cancellation policy. This is clearly an informational message about pickup procedures = B.

A is wrong because the email is about pre-ordered books, not advertising new ones. C is completely unrelated to the email content. D is wrong because the email is about picking up books already ordered, not how to place a new order.
Question 2 — Factual Detail
According to the email, what will happen if a student cancels a pre-order before August 25?
Correct: B Your answer: —
A The student will receive a full cash refund.
B The student will receive store credit. ✓
C The student must exchange the book for a different edition.
D The order will automatically be transferred to the next semester.
Explanation: The passage states: "store credit will be issued for any cancellations made before August 25" = B.

A is a classic trap — the email explicitly says "Refunds are not available for pre-orders," so a cash refund is ruled out. The word "refund" appears in the passage but the answer is NOT a refund. C confuses the exchange policy (for different editions) with the cancellation policy. D is not mentioned anywhere.
Passage 2 Notice
Question 3 — NOT/EXCEPT
According to the notice, all of the following are part of the scheduled maintenance EXCEPT
Correct: C Your answer: —
A inspecting the heating and cooling systems
B replacing fire alarm batteries
C installing new elevator equipment ✓
D repainting common areas
Elimination method:
A — "inspection and repair of the heating and cooling systems" ✓ mentioned
B — "replacement of fire alarm batteries" ✓ mentioned
D — "repainting of all common areas" ✓ mentioned
C — Elevators are never mentioned in the notice. This is the correct answer.

This is a straightforward EXCEPT question. All three distractors use exact wording from the passage, making elimination easy if you read carefully.
Question 4 — Inference
What can be inferred about the basement laundry room during the maintenance period?
Correct: B Your answer: —
A It will be completely closed for the entire week.
B Residents will still be able to use it, but must enter from a different door. ✓
C Only residents on the first floor will have access to it.
D New washing machines will be installed during the maintenance.
Explanation: The notice states: "The basement laundry room will remain open, but access through the south entrance will be restricted. Please use the north entrance instead." From this we can infer that residents can still use it, but need to enter through a different door = B.

A directly contradicts the passage ("will remain open"). C has no basis in the text — no floor restriction is mentioned. D is not part of the listed maintenance work.
